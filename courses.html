Course description

The Spatial Storyteller is an interactive workshop format that teaches and practices storytelling techniques which are necessary for the development of physical and virtual exhibitions.
The workshop helps curators, museum specialists and students to conceptualise the process of telling stories in a physical or virtual space through the use of objects. Over the course of the workshop, participants will implement what they are learning in practice. They will develop their own online 3D interactive exhibition.
Telling a story within an exhibition space and through objects, requires a very different set of writing skills than other media, like essays, books or films. The term spatial storytelling describes the specific linguistic, visual and sensory techniques which are employed by museum professionals during the process of developing an exhibition.

Program details

1

Theory

The theoretical part introduces useful terminology and concepts which are typically used in a professional museum setting.

2

Concepts

The lessons also introduce narrative concepts and practical ways of thinking about the mechanics of storytelling.
Participants will conceptualise the role interpretation plays in creating narratives

3

Examples

The course is based on real world experience of good and bad practises and highlights lessons learnt from actual cases.

4

Practice

The practical part consists of exercises, using an online 3D art programme. The participants will implement what they have learnt throughout the course and build their own online exhibition.

5

Interaction

As the class proceeds, the participants will share their practical experiences, compare challenges and discuss best practices.
